Get Covered Connector

As Enroll America's digital producer, I was responsible for major functionality and UI/UX upgrades to this consumer scheduling and healthcare enrollment data reporting tool (which is also available in a white-label version or as a custom interface). The Connector is now maintained by our friends at Young Invincibles.

Get Covered Plan Explorer

I started at Enroll America just days before the Plan Explorer went live, at the start of the annual open enrollment period, so got to know it intimately through trial by fire. The Plan Explorer was a decision-support tool that allowed consumers to make apples-to-apples comparisons between different health plans in the federal marketplace in a significantly more streamlined manner than HealthCare.gov. Enroll took the tool offline this summer (it is unfortunately unavailable via the Internet Archive because the front end was designed as a JavaScript-based widget, like the Connector above).

vote.colorofchange.org

ColorOfChange built this decision tree (which connected ultimately to Rock the Vote's online voter registration API) to serve and assist potential voters in all 50 states as new, discriminatory voter ID laws went into effect seemingly every few weeks before the 2012 election. My manager challenging me to "break" this site was the first time I ever did UX and QA!

copwatchNYC.org

Without a dedicated design professional on staff at ColorOfChange, I was regularly asked to weigh in on the optimal way to resolve differences of opinion or crossed wires between campaigners, management, our offsite design consultant, and onsite but radically overcommitted team of developers. This project is the one on which I became a believer in product ownership.

colorofchange.org/radio

As ColorOfChange's only campaigner focusing on technology, mass communication, and civil rights, I built out this portal for supporters eligible to apply to the FCC for a community radio broadcasting license almost entirely on my own and within the constraints of our website's existing styling and functionality.

TellYelp.NO

As ColorOfChange's lead campaigner targeting the American Legislative Exchange Council for pushing voter suppression and deadly "Stand Your Ground" gun laws, I conceived, "designed," wrote, and QA'd this personalized greeting for ALEC's newest corporate member. Yelp dropped ALEC in 2014.

Wisconsin Under Walker

I built this infographic detailing some of Scott Walker's many failures as governor for the Madison-based Center for Media and Democracy, as well as national coalition partners including Family Values @ Work and the Economic Policy Institute, in order to shine a spotlight on the disastrous outcome of "Right to Work" laws.

The Other NRA

I created this infographic for the Center for Media and Democracy to support our comrades at Jobs with Justice and ROC United organizing against the National Restaurant Association to win #1FairWage for tipped workers. The tipped (subminimum) wage has been just $2.13/hr for more than 25 years.

Subsidizing Koch

I designed this poster graphic for the Center for Media and Democracy and our partners at Good Jobs First to promote the launch of GJF's subsidy tracker, a tool that makes it easy to learn how much any corporation has received in state or federal subsidies. Koch Industries' haul topped $363 million as of October 2017.
